Pro tip

Everyone makes mistakes. Research is hard and it is messy. So you will make mistakes. The way to respond to a mistake is not to be defensive. You should acknowledge, correct, and move on.

"Now, we scientists … take it for granted that it is perfectly consistent to be unsure — that it is possible to live and not know. But I don’t know whether everyone realizes that this is true. Our freedom to doubt was born of a struggle against authority in the early days of science. It was a very deep and strong struggle. Permit us to question — to doubt, that’s all — not to be sure. And I think it is important that we do not forget the importance of this struggle and thus perhaps lose what we have gained. Here lies a responsibility to society." - Feynman
